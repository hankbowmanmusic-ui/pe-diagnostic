Step 3 of 6
Deal Execution Effectiveness

Average B2B win rates are 20–25%. Best-in-class organizations achieve 40%+. The gap is almost always process and management, not product.

Industry Reality
Forecast accuracy reveals execution gaps more reliably than pipeline size. "No decision" rates and competitive displacement patterns are the most diagnostic — and most ignored — sales metrics.
W.1 — Competitive Win Rate
What is the current win rate on competitive opportunities (deals where you were evaluated against at least one alternative)?
Industry Benchmark: 20–25% average · Best-in-class: 40%+
%
W.2 — Forecast Accuracy
How accurate is the sales forecast at the start of each quarter vs. actual close?
Best-in-class: within ±5% · Most companies: ±20–30%

W.4 — "No Decision" Rate

A high no-decision rate often signals a value articulation or urgency problem, not a competitive problem. It's a different fix than a low win rate.

Lens 03 — GROW.
Step 4 of 6
Customer Expansion Systems

Hidden churn patterns silently destroy lifetime value calculations and growth projections. Most portfolio companies don't discover this until it's too late in the hold period.

Industry Reality
Net Dollar Retention above 110% is the single most powerful predictor of scalable growth. Companies below 90% NDR are running on a treadmill — new revenue replaces lost revenue rather than stacking.
G.1 — Net Dollar Retention (NDR)
What is current Net Dollar Retention — existing customer revenue this year vs. same cohort last year, including expansions and churn?
Benchmark: 90% floor · Growth-grade: 100–110% · Best-in-class: 120%+
% NDR
G.2 — Churn Pattern Visibility
How clearly do you understand when and why customers churn — with root cause data, not anecdote?
No systematic analysisFull cohort + root cause data
G.3 — Multi-Product Penetration
What percentage of customers use more than one product or service line?
Multi-product adoption is the strongest churn protection and the highest-margin expansion path. It's often the most underleveraged asset in the portfolio.
G.4 — Account Management Process Maturity
How mature is your account management motion — QBRs, health scoring, expansion playbooks, executive relationship coverage?
Reactive / relationship-basedSystematic expansion engine
G.5 — Share of Wallet Analysis
For your top 20 accounts, do you have a documented understanding of total addressable spend — and what percentage you currently capture?
No visibility into wallet shareFully mapped and pursued
