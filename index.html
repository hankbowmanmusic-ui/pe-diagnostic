Step 3 of 6
Deal Execution Effectiveness

Average B2B win rates are 20–25%. Best-in-class organizations achieve 40%+. The gap is almost always process and management discipline — not product.

Industry Reality
Forecast accuracy reveals execution gaps more reliably than pipeline size. "No decision" rates and deal slippage patterns are the most diagnostic — and most commonly ignored — metrics in portfolio company sales reviews.
W.1 — Competitive Win Rate
What is the current win rate on competitive opportunities — deals where you were evaluated against at least one alternative?
Industry Average: 20–25% · Best-in-class: 40%+
%
W.2 — Forecast Accuracy

Best-in-class: within ±5% · Most companies: ±20–30%

W.4 — "No Decision" Rate
What percentage of qualified pipeline ends in "no decision" — the prospect decides not to buy from anyone?
A high no-decision rate signals a value articulation or urgency problem — not a competitive one. It's a different fix than a low win rate.

Lens 03 — GROW.
Step 4 of 6
Customer Expansion Systems

Hidden churn patterns silently destroy lifetime value calculations and exit multiples. Most portfolio companies don't discover them until it's too late in the hold period.

Industry Reality
Net Dollar Retention above 110% is the single most powerful predictor of scalable growth. Companies below 90% NDR are running on a treadmill — new revenue replaces lost revenue rather than stacking on top of it.
G.1 — Net Dollar Retention (NDR)
What is current Net Dollar Retention — existing customer revenue this year vs. same cohort last year, including expansions, contractions, and churn?
Floor: 90% · Growth-grade: 100–110% · Best-in-class: 120%+
% NDR
G.2 — Churn Pattern Visibility
How clearly do you understand when and why customers churn — with root cause data, not anecdote?
No systematic analysisFull cohort + root cause data
G.3 — Multi-Product Penetration
What percentage of customers use more than one product or service line?
Multi-product adoption is the strongest churn protection and the highest-margin expansion path. It's often the most underleveraged asset in a PE portfolio company.
G.4 — Account Management Maturity
How mature is your account management motion — QBRs, health scoring, expansion playbooks, executive relationship coverage?
Reactive / relationship-basedSystematic expansion engine
G.5 — Share of Wallet Analysis
